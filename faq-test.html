Q1

What is a penetration test?

A penetration test is a simulated cyber attack against your system to identify vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malicious actors. Unlike automated scans, it involves manual testing, creative exploitation, and comprehensive analysis of security weaknesses across your infrastructure.

Q2

How often should I get tested?

Annually at minimum, or after significant changes to your infrastructure, application updates, new features, or following security incidents. High-risk industries or rapidly evolving products benefit from quarterly assessments. Bug bounty programs provide continuous testing.

Q3

What happens after testing?

You receive a detailed report with findings, risk ratings, proof-of-concept demonstrations, and remediation guidance. We can schedule a debrief call to walk through results. Optional retesting available after fixes are implemented to verify proper remediation.

Q4

What if you find critical issues?

Critical findings are reported immediately via secure channels. I provide emergency remediation guidance and can work with your team to implement urgent fixes. All critical issues are verified fixed through retesting at no additional cost within the engagement period.
